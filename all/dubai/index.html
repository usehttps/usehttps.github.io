Dubai

🇦🇪 Must-Visit Places in Dubai

Iconic Landmarks:

  • Burj Khalifa – World’s tallest tower; visit at sunset for stunning skyline views.

  • Dubai Fountain – Watch the evening fountain shows outside Dubai Mall.

  • Palm Jumeirah – Man-made island; visit The View at The Palm or Atlantis Aquaventure Waterpark.

  • Dubai Marina – Scenic waterfront with restaurants and yacht cruises.

  • Jumeirah Beach / Kite Beach – Relax with Burj Al Arab in the background.

  • Al Fahidi Historical District (Al Bastakiya) – Explore old Dubai charm and museums.

  • Dubai Frame – A massive picture frame showing “Old” vs “New” Dubai.

  • Desert Safari – 4x4 dune bashing, camel rides, and BBQ dinner.

Modern & Cultural Gems:

  • Museum of the Future – Futuristic architecture and immersive exhibits.

  • Dubai Miracle Garden – Seasonal (Nov–Apr) floral wonderland.

  • Global Village – Seasonal (Oct–Apr) multicultural food and entertainment park.

  • Dubai Creek Harbour – Great sunset views and calm vibes.

🍴 Must-Eat in Dubai

Local / Emirati Cuisine:

  • Al Machboos – Spiced rice with meat or seafood.

  • Luqaimat – Sweet fried dough balls with date syrup.

  • Harees – Slow-cooked wheat and meat dish.

  • Regag Bread – Thin crispy bread often served with eggs or cheese.

Street Food & Popular Picks:

  • Shawarma – Found everywhere; try Al Mallah (Satwa) or Automatic.

  • Manakish – Lebanese flatbread with cheese or za’atar.

  • Falafel – Fresh and crispy; try Operation: Falafel.

  • Karak Chai – Spiced milk tea; found in tea kiosks across the city.

High-End Dining:

  • Dinner in the Sky – Unique elevated dining experience.

  • Pierchic – Romantic dining over the sea.

  • Ossiano (Atlantis) – Michelin-starred underwater restaurant.

🚇 Public Transport Guide to Dubai

Main Transport Options

  1. Dubai Metro – Clean, safe, and punctual. Red Line & Green Line connect major areas.

  2. Dubai Tram – Serves Marina, JBR, and Al Sufouh areas.

  3. Public Buses – Cover most areas; air-conditioned and affordable.

  4. Water Taxis & Abras – Traditional boats across Dubai Creek (AED 1–2 only!).

  5. Taxis & Ride Apps – Careem and Uber widely available.

How to Use

  • Get an NOL Card (rechargeable smart card) from any metro station or vending machine.

  • Tap in and out on buses, trams, and metro.

  • Use the RTA Dubai app or Google Maps for routes and fare estimates.

Airport to City Transport

  • Dubai Metro (Red Line) connects DXB Airport Terminals 1 & 3 to major areas like Burj Khalifa/Dubai Mall, Mall of the Emirates, and Dubai Marina.

  • Taxi from airport to Downtown ≈ AED 60–80.

  • Airport Bus available but slower.

💡 Tips and Tricks for Transportation

  • Buy an NOL Silver Card (AED 25 with AED 19 credit).

  • Avoid rush hour: 7–9 AM & 5–7 PM.

  • Women-only metro cabins available.

  • Weekend = Friday–Sunday, so Thursday evenings are busy.

  • Abra rides are scenic and cheap — don’t miss one across Dubai Creek.

  • Use Day Pass (AED 22) for unlimited rides (great for tourists).

💰 Budget Traveler Tips

  • Stay in Al Barsha, Deira, or Bur Dubai (cheap + near metro).

  • Eat at local cafeterias or mall food courts.

  • Use NOL daily pass for unlimited metro/bus.

  • Visit free attractions: beaches, Marina Walk, Souks, Dubai Fountain show.

  • Book Burj Khalifa tickets online in advance for cheaper rates.

💞 Honeymoon in Dubai

  • Stay at Atlantis, Burj Al Arab, or Jumeirah Zabeel Saray.

  • Romantic dinners: Pierchic, Ossiano, Dinner in the Sky.

  • Couple spa at Talise Spa, Jumeirah Beach Hotel.

  • Sunset yacht cruise at Dubai Marina.

🕌 Major Annual Festivals & Events

  • Dubai Shopping Festival (Dec–Jan): Huge discounts, concerts, and fireworks.

  • Dubai Food Festival (Feb–Mar): Street food markets & celebrity chef events.

  • Ramadan (dates vary yearly): Holy month — quieter days, but beautiful iftars at night.

  • National Day (Dec 2): Patriotic parades, lights, and shows.

  • Global Village (Oct–Apr): Cultural pavilions, food, and entertainment.

  • Dubai Expo City events: Year-round fairs and tech exhibitions.

🕶️ Worst Time to Visit:
June–September — scorching heat and humidity make outdoor sightseeing difficult, though flights and hotels are cheapest then.

🛡️ Safety Level in Dubai

✅ Overall Safety:

  • Extremely safe for tourists — among the lowest crime rates globally.

  • Strong police presence, CCTV coverage, and strict laws maintain order.

🚫 Things to Be Aware Of:

  • No violent crime, but petty theft can happen in crowded tourist spots.

  • Follow local laws: Public drunkenness, PDA, and rude gestures can lead to fines or arrest.

  • Political stability: Very stable and peaceful.

Solo travelers and families can walk safely at night in most areas (even Downtown or Deira).

💰 Currency and Exchange Rate in Dubai

  • Currency: 🇦🇪 AED – Arab Emirates Dirham

  • Exchange rate (approx.):

    • 1 USD ≈ 3.67 AED (fixed rate)

    • 1 EUR ≈ 3.95 AED

    • 1 GBP ≈ 4.65 AED

💵 Money Tips:

  • Exchange at authorized exchange centers (Al Ansari, UAE Exchange, Travelex).

  • Credit cards widely accepted.

  • ATMs available everywhere — small fee may apply.

  • Always carry small cash for taxis, markets, and tips.

🗣️ Languages Spoken in Dubai

  • Official: Arabic

  • Commonly Spoken: English (widely used in business, hotels, restaurants, and taxis)

  • Other languages often heard: Hindi, Urdu, Tagalog, Persian.
    ➡️ No language barrier for English speakers.

💡 Ways to Save Money in Dubai

💸 Transport:

  • Use NOL Card for metro/tram/bus instead of taxis.

  • Use Daily Pass (AED 22) for unlimited public transport.

🎟️ Sightseeing Passes:

  • Dubai Explorer Pass (Go City) – discounted entry to top attractions.

  • Dubai Pass by iVenture – choose 3–5 attractions, save up to 50%.

  • RTA Day Pass – for unlimited metro/tram/bus rides.

🍴 Food & Shopping:

  • Eat at cafeterias in Karama, Satwa, or Al Barsha.

  • Buy souvenirs at local souks (bargain politely).

  • Avoid buying gold/souvenirs in malls — go to Gold Souk.

🏨 Accommodation:

  • Stay in Bur Dubai, Deira, or Al Barsha for cheaper hotels with metro access.

  • Avoid peak festival weeks for better rates.

🎭 Seasonal & Cultural Events

  • Jan–Feb: Dubai Shopping Festival

  • Mar: Dubai Food Festival

  • Apr–May: Ramadan & Eid al-Fitr (beautiful night markets)

  • Oct–Apr: Global Village

  • Nov: Dubai Airshow, Design Week

  • Dec: National Day & New Year Fireworks (Burj Khalifa + Palm Jumeirah)

⚠️ Common Tourist Scams to Avoid

Dubai is very safe, but stay smart:

  1. Fake Gold or Perfume Sellers: Only buy from reputable souks or stores.

  2. Overpriced Taxi Rides: Use official RTA taxis (cream color) or Careem app.

  3. “Free Tour” or “Free Safari” Scams: Always book through licensed agencies.

  4. Fake Charity Solicitations: Avoid street collectors; donate only through official platforms.

  5. ATM “Helpers”: Never accept help entering your PIN.

  6. Timeshare Sales in Malls: Politely decline; they can waste your time.
