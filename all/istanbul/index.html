Istanbul

Must-Visit in Istanbul

Historical & Cultural Sites

  • Hagia Sophia (Ayasofya) – Iconic Byzantine landmark, now a museum/mosque.

  • Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Camii) – Stunning architecture, don’t miss the evening lighting.

  • Topkapi Palace – Home of Ottoman sultans, with beautiful courtyards and harem.

  • Basilica Cistern – Ancient underground water reservoir, very atmospheric.

  • Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı) – One of the world’s largest covered markets.

  • Spice Bazaar (Mısır Çarşısı) – Colorful market for spices, sweets, and souvenirs.

  • Galata Tower – Panoramic city views.

  • Süleymaniye Mosque – Architecturally magnificent, less crowded than Blue Mosque.

  • Chora Church (Kariye Museum) – Byzantine mosaics and frescoes.

Modern & Trendy Areas

  • Istiklal Street & Taksim Square – Shopping, cafés, nightlife.

  • Karaköy – Cool neighborhood with street art, bakeries, and cafés.

  • Kadıköy (Asian side) – Vibrant local scene, markets, and food spots.

  • Ortaköy – Bosphorus view, famous for kumpir (stuffed baked potatoes).

Scenic Spots

  • Bosphorus Cruise – Short or full-day cruise to see palaces and bridges.

  • Pierre Loti Hill – Sunset views over the Golden Horn.

  • Çamlıca Hill – Panoramic views from the Asian side.

Must-Eat in Istanbul

Street Food & Snacks

  • Simit – Sesame-covered bread ring.

  • Balık Ekmek – Fish sandwich by the Galata Bridge.

  • Kokoreç – Grilled lamb intestines (for adventurous eaters).

  • Midye Dolma – Stuffed mussels.

  • Lahmacun – Turkish pizza.

Restaurants & Local Cuisine

  • Meze & Kebabs – Try at a local meyhane (tavern).

  • Baklava & Turkish Delight – Hafiz Mustafa or Karaköy Güllüoğlu.

  • Pide – Turkish flatbread with toppings.

  • Çorba – Traditional soups, especially lentil soup.

Public Transport in Istanbul

Main Options

  • Metro – Fastest way to cross long distances (lines M1, M2, M4, etc.).

  • Tram – Good for tourist areas (T1 line covers Sultanahmet to Kabataş).

  • Bus / Minibus – Extensive coverage but can be slower in traffic.

  • Ferry / Sea Bus – Connects European and Asian sides; scenic.

  • Marmaray – Undersea rail tunnel linking Europe and Asia.

  • Funicular – Short rides connecting metro/tram to hills or ferry terminals.

How to Use

  • Buy an Istanbulkart (reloadable smart card) at metro stations, kiosks, or convenience stores.

  • Tap in/out on buses, metro, trams, ferries.

  • Single ride fares are ~15–20 TL, but Istanbulkart reduces cost.

From Airports

  • Istanbul Airport (IST): Metro M11 (under construction; shuttle buses also connect) or Havaist buses to city center.

  • Sabiha Gökçen (SAW): Havaist buses to Kadıköy or Taksim; taxi is faster but more expensive.

Tips & Tricks

  • Avoid rush hours (8–10 AM, 5–7 PM).

  • Ferries are scenic and often faster than land transport.

  • Keep cash for buses/minibuses if you run out of Istanbulkart credit.

  • Download apps: Moovit or Trafi for real-time transit info.

Sample Itineraries

3 Nights

Day 1: Sultanahmet – Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Basilica Cistern.
Day 2: Topkapi Palace, Grand Bazaar, Spice Bazaar, Bosphorus cruise.
Day 3: Galata Tower, Istiklal Street, Taksim, and Karaköy.

5 Nights

  • Add Asian Side (Kadıköy, Üsküdar), Süleymaniye Mosque, Ortaköy, Chora Church.

  • Optional Bosphorus full-day tour or Princes’ Islands day trip.

10 Nights

  • Deep dive into neighborhoods, museums (Istanbul Modern, Rahmi Koç), day trips:

    • Princes’ Islands

    • Edirne (historical Ottoman city)

    • Bursa (thermal baths, silk market)

Budget Traveler Tips

  • Stay in Sultanahmet or Beyoğlu for walking access to sites.

  • Eat street food & local cafés to save money.

  • Use Istanbulkart for cheap transit.

  • Many museums have free entrance days (check schedule).

  • Avoid taxis; public transport and walking are enough.

Honeymoon Ideas

  • Romantic Bosphorus sunset cruise.

  • Boutique hotel in Galata or Sultanahmet with Bosphorus view.

  • Couples’ hammam experience (Çemberlitaş or Ayasofya Hürrem Sultan Hamam).

  • Private dining by the Bosphorus.

Family Trip with Kids

  • Visit Miniatürk – miniature models of Turkey.

  • Istanbul Aquarium or Sea Life.

  • Take ferries instead of long buses.

  • Avoid late-night activities; stick to parks and playful areas.

  • Bring stroller-friendly route maps; some historic sites have cobblestones.

Best Time to Visit Istanbul

Weather & Seasons

  • Spring (April–June): Ideal weather; mild temperatures (15–25°C), blooming gardens.

  • Autumn (September–November): Pleasant, less crowded, colorful foliage, 15–25°C.

  • Summer (July–August): Hot (25–35°C), humid, heavy tourist crowds.

  • Winter (December–February): Cold (5–10°C), occasional snow, some attractions may close, fewer tourists.

Festivals & Events

  • Istanbul Film Festival (April)

  • Tulip Festival (April) – Parks like Emirgan are spectacular.

  • Istanbul Music Festival (June)

  • Ramadan / Eid Celebrations – Unique cultural experience, dates vary.

  • Istanbul Biennial (September–November) – Contemporary art.

Worst Season to Avoid: High summer (July–August) due to heat, crowds, and expensive accommodation.

Safety Level

  • Crime: Generally safe for tourists. Pickpocketing can happen in crowded areas (Grand Bazaar, Taksim).

  • Political Stability: Stable, but avoid political demonstrations; check local news.

  • Scams: Common tourist scams include fake taxi meters, “friendship bracelet” schemes, overcharging in markets.

Tips: Always use licensed taxis, Istanbulkart for transport, and keep personal belongings secure.

Internet Access & SIM Cards

  • Free Wi-Fi: Available in hotels, cafés, and some public areas (airports, malls).

  • SIM Cards: Buy a tourist SIM at the airport or local shops. Popular providers:

    • Turkcell, Vodafone, Türk Telekom

  • Data plans: 10–15 GB for ~300–400 TL, valid 30 days.

  • eSIM: Some providers support instant activation.

Currency & Exchange Rate

  • Currency: Turkish Lira (TRY)

  • Exchange rate: ~1 USD ≈ 55–60 TRY (varies, check before travel).

  • Tips: Use ATMs for best rates; credit cards widely accepted.

Language

  • Official: Turkish

  • English: Common in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants.

  • Useful Phrase: “Teşekkür ederim” = Thank you, “Merhaba” = Hello

Ways to Save Money

  • Istanbulkart: Reduces public transport cost.

  • Museum Pass Istanbul: Includes top museums, cheaper than single tickets.

  • Markets & Street Food: Cheaper than restaurants in tourist areas.

  • Early Booking: Hotels and ferries.

  • Walking: Many attractions are close in Sultanahmet/Taksim.

Seasonal & Cultural Events

  • Tulip Festival (April) – Emirgan Park & city gardens

  • Ramadan & Eid: Street festivities and markets

  • Istanbul Biennial (Art, Sept–Nov)

  • Istanbul Coffee Festival (Dec) – For coffee lovers

Power Plug & Voltage

  • Plug Type: Type C & F (two round pins)

  • Voltage: 230V, 50Hz

  • Tip: Bring universal adapter if your devices have different plugs.

Emergency Numbers & Hospitals

  • Ambulance / Fire / Police: 112

  • Tourist Police: 155

  • Major Hospitals:

    • Acıbadem Hospital (multiple locations)

    • American Hospital Istanbul

    • Florence Nightingale Hospital

Common Tourist Scams

  1. Overpriced Taxis: Insist on meter usage or use ride apps (BiTaksi, Uber).

  2. “Friendship Bracelet” Scam: Strangers tie a bracelet and demand money.

  3. Fake Guides: Only use licensed guides or hotel recommendations.

  4. Money Exchange: Avoid street exchangers; use banks or ATMs.

  5. Restaurant Tricks: Be careful of hidden service charges; always check the menu.
