Rome

🏛️ Must-Visit Places in Rome

Classic Highlights

  1. Colosseum & Roman Forum – Ancient Rome’s heart; book a combo ticket and go early.

  2. Pantheon – Nearly 2,000 years old and still jaw-dropping.

  3. Trevi Fountain – Toss a coin to ensure your return to Rome.

  4. Piazza Navona – Baroque fountains and lively street artists.

  5. Spanish Steps – Iconic photo spot and shopping area.

  6. Vatican City – Visit St. Peter’s Basilica, Vatican Museums, and the Sistine Chapel.

  7. Trastevere – Cobblestone lanes, local trattorias, and authentic nightlife.

  8. Castel Sant’Angelo – Great sunset views over the Tiber River.

  9. Villa Borghese Gardens – Peaceful park with Galleria Borghese art museum.

  10. Campo de’ Fiori & Testaccio – Markets and true Roman life.

🍝 Must-Eat Foods in Rome

Classic Roman Dishes

  • Cacio e Pepe – Pecorino cheese and black pepper pasta.

  • Carbonara – Egg, guanciale (pork cheek), and pecorino (no cream!).

  • Amatriciana – Tomato, guanciale, and pecorino.

  • Supplì – Fried rice balls with mozzarella.

  • Carciofi alla Romana – Artichokes cooked Roman-style.

  • Saltimbocca alla Romana – Veal with prosciutto and sage.

  • Pizza al Taglio – Roman square pizza slices sold by weight.

  • Gelato – Try Giolitti, Fatamorgana, or Gelateria del Teatro.

  • Espresso – Stand at the bar like locals do.

🚇 Public Transport in Rome

Main Options

  • Metro: 3 lines (A, B/B1, C) – simple and fast for main sights.

  • Bus & Tram: Extensive but often slow due to traffic.

  • Regional Trains: Connect suburbs and airports.

  • Taxi or Ride Apps: Official taxis (white, “TAXI” sign) or apps like FreeNow or ItTaxi.

  • Walking: Often faster in the city center!

Tickets

  • €1.50 for 100 minutes on bus/tram/metro (BIT ticket).

  • €7 for 24-hour pass, €12.50 for 48 h, €18 for 72 h, €24 for weekly (CIS).

  • Buy from metro stations, tabacchi (tobacco shops), or ATAC mobile app.

How to Use

  1. Validate your ticket once (in bus/tram or metro gate).

  2. Keep it until your trip ends.

  3. Inspectors do random checks – fines are heavy (€100+).

🛫 Public Transport from and to Rome Airports

From Fiumicino (FCO):

  • 🚆 Leonardo Express Train → Termini Station (32 min, €14).

  • 🚆 FL1 Train → Trastevere/Tiburtina (cheaper, €8).

  • 🚌 Shuttle Bus → Termini (45 – 60 min, €6–7).

  • 🚕 Taxi (fixed rate) €50 to central Rome.

From Ciampino (CIA):

  • 🚌 Shuttle Bus to Termini Station (40 min, €6).

  • 🚕 Taxi fixed rate €31 to central Rome.

💡 Transport Tips & Tricks

  • Avoid rush hours (7–9 am, 5–7 pm) – metros get crowded.

  • Google Maps or Moovit works well for route planning.

  • Keep small coins for bus ticket machines (some don’t take cards).

  • Watch for pickpockets on buses and metros (especially Line A).

  • Rome Pass or Omnia Card can save time & money if visiting many attractions.

🗓️ Itinerary Ideas

3-Night (4-Day) Rome Itinerary

Day 1: Colosseum → Roman Forum → Capitoline Hill → Piazza Venezia → Trevi Fountain
Day 2: Vatican Museums → Sistine Chapel → St. Peter’s Basilica → Castel Sant’Angelo → Trastevere dinner
Day 3: Pantheon → Piazza Navona → Campo de’ Fiori → Villa Borghese → Galleria Borghese
Day 4 (morning): Local café + shopping at Spanish Steps

5-Night (6-Day) Itinerary

Add to the above:

  • Day 4: Day trip to Tivoli (Villa d’Este & Hadrian’s Villa) or Ostia Antica.

  • Day 5: Explore Testaccio food market, Appian Way (Via Appia Antica) by bike, and Catacombs.

10-Night (11-Day) Itinerary

  • Include all above +

  • Day 6–7: Overnight trip to Florence or Naples (1–1.5 h by train).

  • Day 8: Explore Jewish Ghetto & Trastevere more deeply.

  • Day 9: Cooking class or wine tasting near Rome.

  • Day 10: Relax at Villa Borghese or go shopping on Via del Corso.

💶 Budget Traveler’s Rome

Accommodation: Hostels or budget guesthouses near Termini or Trastevere.
Eat cheap: Pizzerias al taglio, trattorie, or aperitivo buffets.
Free sights: Pantheon, Trevi Fountain, Spanish Steps, Piazzas, and churches (many have Michelangelo or Caravaggio works!).
Transport: Use multi-day ATAC pass.
Museum tip: First Sunday of each month – free state museum entry.

💕 Honeymoon in Rome

  • Stay in Trastevere, Monti, or near Piazza Navona.

  • Romantic spots: Pincio Terrace, Giardino degli Aranci (Orange Garden), Tiber River cruise.

  • Candle-light dinners: Aroma (Colosseum view), Il Margutta, or La Pergola.

  • Day trip ideas: Tivoli Gardens or Castel Gandolfo for lakeside romance.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Trip with Small Kids

  • Stay near Villa Borghese (green spaces, zoo, playgrounds).

  • Attractions for kids: Explora Children’s Museum, Bioparco Zoo, Time Elevator Rome, and Gelato tastings!

  • Many piazzas are stroller-friendly; avoid cobblestone alleys with narrow wheels.

  • Family restaurants: Tonnarello, Pizzeria da Baffetto, La Montecarlo (kid-friendly, fast service).

  • Keep baby wipes and snacks handy — bathrooms can be sparse.

🎉 Major Festivals & Events

  • Easter (Mar/Apr) – Vatican ceremonies and processions.

  • April 21 – Natale di Roma – Founding of Rome parade and fireworks.

  • June – Estate Romana – Summer concerts and outdoor events.

  • July – Festa de’ Noantri (Trastevere) – Folk festival.

  • October – Rome Film Festival – International event at Auditorium Parco della Musica.

  • December – Christmas Markets at Piazza Navona, Vatican tree lighting.

🛡️ Safety Level in Rome

  • Crime: Generally safe, especially central areas (Vatican, Trastevere, Monti).

  • Main issues: Pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and public transport (Metro Line A, Termini, buses).

  • Political stability: Very stable and peaceful.

  • Tips:

    • Keep your bag zipped and in front of you.

    • Avoid people offering “free bracelets” or “help with photos.”

    • Use ATMs inside banks, not on the street.

    • Don’t leave bags unattended at restaurants or fountains.

Emergency line: 🇪🇺 112 (EU-wide emergency number).

💶 Currency & Exchange Rate in Rome

  • Currency: Euro (€)

  • Exchange rate (approx, Oct 2025): €1 = US $1.07 / SEK 11.70

  • Payment: Cards widely accepted (Visa, Mastercard). Small cafés may prefer cash.

  • ATMs: Everywhere; use bank ATMs for best rates (avoid “Euronet”).

  • Tipping: Optional – round up the bill or leave 5–10% for great service.

🗣️ Languages Spoken in Rome

  • Official: Italian 🇮🇹

  • English: Widely understood in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants.

  • Useful phrases:

    • Hello – Ciao / Buongiorno

    • Thank you – Grazie

    • Please – Per favore

    • Excuse me – Scusi

    • How much? – Quanto costa?

💰 Ways to Save Money in Rome

🎟️ Passes & Discounts

  • Roma Pass (48 h – €33, 72 h – €53): Free entry to 1–2 museums, free public transport, discounts at others.

  • Omnia Card (€129): Includes Vatican Museums + Roma Pass combo.

  • Archeologia Card: Access to several archaeological sites.

  • Free Sunday (first Sunday each month): Free entry to state museums and ruins.

🍕 Local Tips

  • Eat at trattorias or osterias instead of tourist restaurants.

  • Have aperitivo (drink + snacks) instead of dinner some nights.

  • Buy fruit and sandwiches at local markets (Campo de’ Fiori, Testaccio Market).

  • Walk or use ATAC transport passes instead of taxis.

⚠️ Common Tourist Scams to Avoid in Rome

  1. Bracelet Trick: Someone ties a “free bracelet” on your wrist, then demands payment.

  2. Photo Helpers: People offering to take your photo for free — then ask for money.

  3. Fake Petition / Deaf Charity: People hand you clipboards; distracts for pickpockets.

  4. Overpriced Taxis: Always use licensed taxis (white with “TAXI” sign, meter on).

  5. ATM Skimmers: Use ATMs inside banks.

  6. Restaurant Service Fees: Check for coperto or servizio before sitting down.

  7. Fake Ticket Sellers: Buy Colosseum/Vatican tickets only from official sites or vendors.

  8. Pickpockets on Metro: Especially around Termini, Barberini, and Colosseo stations.
